## Context

The early-1990s stretch of the long-run GDP series is the part the post itself flags as
least certain. `R/prepare_gdp.R` marks 1991-1995 as `break_period` ("reconstructed, not
observed national accounts"), and the GDP plan calls the depth of the 1990s fall "the single
number to trust least." The reconstruction is annual (Maddison growth rates), so we never see
*when* inside the year the economy fell, and we have no observed hard indicator standing next
to the reconstructed numbers.

The author supplied `for Chow-Lin.png` — an HNB scan of **Tablica 2, "Razina industrijske
proizvodnje" (stope rasta)**, monthly industrial-production growth for **1991-01 to 1992-12**,
*Izvor: Podaci DZS obrađeni u NBH*. Industrial production is an observed, high-frequency
indicator that moves with GDP. The task: transcribe it, run a **Chow-Lin temporal
disaggregation** of annual GDP to monthly using this indicator, and use the result as a
**common-sense check** on the existing annual reconstruction — does the IPI-guided monthly path,
and its implied annual fall, sit consistently with the Maddison-based 1991/1992 numbers?

Decisions confirmed with the author: compare against the **annual GDP reconstruction** (and show
GDP vs industry side by side); disaggregate to **monthly**.

## Target series (the "existing" reconstruction)

Annual GDP per capita, Maddison (2011 int'l $), from `data/processed/gdp_raw.csv`:
1990 = 12948, **1991 = 10296 (-20.5%)**, **1992 = 9332 (-9.4%)**. Industry fell harder
(-28.3%, then -14.8%) — the expected pattern (industry is more cyclical than services/agriculture).
Per-capita units and the within-year-constant population are fine here: the check is about shape
and magnitude, and everything is shown indexed to 1990=100. (Caveat noted in the writeup.)

## Method

Chow-Lin (1971): annual `GDP = a + b*IPI + u`, high-frequency `u ~ AR(1)`; GLS for `a, b`, then the
BLUE distribution back to monthly subject to the annual totals (`conversion = "sum"`, GDP is a flow).

**Honest note on identification.** We only have the indicator for 1991-1992, so there are exactly
**two annual benchmarks and two regression parameters** — the model is exactly identified, the two
annual residuals are zero, and the AR(1) term drops out. Chow-Lin then reduces to the indicator
**linearly rescaled to hit the two annual totals**: `GDP_m = a + b*IPI_m` with
`b = (G1991 - G1992)/(S1991 - S1992)`, `a = (G1991 - b*S1991)/12` (S = sum of the 12 monthly IPI).
This is genuinely the Chow-Lin estimator in this case, not a shortcut, and it is exactly what a
common-sense check wants: spread annual GDP along the observed industrial profile, then look at the
result. The fitted intercept `a` (the part of GDP that does **not** move with industry — services,
agriculture, public sector) is itself an economic sanity check.

Primary implementation: a transparent, self-contained R routine (no new package needed). If
`tempdisagg` is installable from CRAN, additionally run `td(gdp ~ ipi, conversion="sum",
method="chow-lin-maxlog")` as a cross-check and assert the two agree. If it is not available, skip
the cross-check and say so in the writeup — the result does not depend on it.
