# Short. Sharp. Reproducible.

House style for turning an approved data draft into a finished post. The draft brings the numbers. You bring the angle. The reader should finish in two minutes and walk away holding three numbers.

## One mission

Cut the distance from data to insight. Every post is a short, purposeful, reproducible read. Nothing decorative. Nothing hedged. Just the finding and the proof.

## The voice

Confident and kinetic. You are a sharp analyst telling a colleague what you just found, not a committee filing a report. Present tense. Active voice. The data talks and you point at it.

The house also keeps its punctuation clean. Periods over colons. Arrows over dashes. Italics over quote marks. Parentheses for the quick aside. Fewer marks, more momentum.

## How a sentence should land

One idea per sentence. One point per paragraph. Lead with the finding, then show the proof. A fragment can hit harder than a full sentence, so reach for fragments on purpose.

> 22 godine, dvije priče.

> Realokacija u jednoj slici.

> Firme su preživljavale. Rezale su ljude.

Look at that last pair. Two short clauses, two periods, no dash. Subject, verb, punch.

## Lead with the number

The number is the hero, so put it early. Show change over time with an arrow rather than words.

> Broj firmi. 65.000 (2002.) → 162.000 (2024.).

> Turizam udvostručuje. 45.000 → 96.000.

Round so the number sticks. 162.000, never 161.847. Keep the house format steady. Period as the thousands mark, the trailing period on years, the arrow for any change. Put the magnitude in parentheses right after the claim, written as plus or minus in words so no stray dash creeps in.

> zdravstvo plus 164%, industrija minus 5%

## Headlines in two beats

Beat one hooks. Beat two states the finding in plain words. The hook can be vivid. The second line must be literal, so even a skimmer leaves with the result.

> Firme rastu, radna mjesta se sele
> Broj firmi udvostručen, a radna mjesta su iz industrije i građevine pobjegla u turizam i usluge

## Headers that carry the story

Write every section header as a claim that stands on its own. Stack the headers top to bottom and you should read the whole article in miniature.

> Recesija se vidi u radnim mjestima, ne u broju firmi

> Industrija drži vrh, građevina pada i ustaje, turizam eksplodira

If a header names only a topic, rewrite it as a finding.

## Verbs that move

Choose verbs with motion and weight. Make the economy feel alive on the page.

> radna mjesta su pobjegla u turizam

> turizam eksplodira

> građevina pada i ustaje

Save the big words for the big moves. A collapse of 37% earns the word drama. A dip of 2% does not.

## Winners and losers

Readers remember contrast, so sort the world into who gained and who lost.

> Dobitnici. Zdravstvo plus 164%, turizam plus 113%, informacije plus 74%.
> Gubitnici. Rudarstvo minus 49%, nekretnine minus 40%, financije minus 37%.

## Chart captions

Give every chart one short caption that says plainly what the reader is looking at.

> Indeks (2008. = 100). Broj firmi i ukupan broj zaposlenih.

> Promjena zaposlenosti po djelatnosti, 2008. do 2024.

## Coining a phrase

When you name an idea, set it in italics rather than quote marks. It reads cleaner and keeps the house look.

> Logika *živih mrtvaca*. Poduzeća ostaju, ljudi odlaze.

## Breaking the dash habit

Drafts arrive with long dashes propping open every aside. Strip them out. You have three clean replacements at hand. Split the sentence in two. Use an arrow for change over time. Use parentheses for a quick aside. Here is the same fact rebuilt the house way.

> Građevina je drama. 118.000 → 74.000 (minus 37%). Pa natrag na 135.000.

Same facts, more punch, nothing left hanging.

## Editorial markers

Tag every spot that needs human judgment with [KUT]. KUT is your angle, the reading a script can never produce. Leave the number to the pipeline. Leave the meaning to you.

> [KUT] Što ova realokacija govori o hrvatskom modelu rasta. Zdrav strukturni pomak ili ovisnost o turizmu.

Resolve or delete every [KUT] before publishing. None survive into the final post.

## The method box

Close every post with method and limits. Short, technical, honest. Name the source, the table, the columns, the scripts. Flag whatever is not yet trustworthy. This is the reproducible promise of the blog, so it never gets skipped.

> Izvor. GFI baza, tablica `db_afs`, jedan redak po firmi i godini.
> Zaposleni. `employeecounteop`, zbroj po djelatnosti.
> Oprez. Rast broja firmi dijelom odražava širi obuhvat baze. Zaposlenost je robustnija mjera.

Set table names, column names, and script paths in backticks so they read as code, not prose.

## What kills the punch

Hedging. *Općenito govoreći, čini se da.* Cut it and say the thing.

Throat clearing. *Vrijedi napomenuti da.* Just napomeni.

Passive voice where active works. *Radna mjesta su izgubljena* becomes *Firme su rezale ljude.*

Vague attribution. *Stručnjaci kažu.* Name the source or drop the claim.

Adjectives with no number behind them. *Značajan rast* says nothing. *Plus 113%* says everything.

Stacked dashes. You already know the fix.

## Before you publish

1. The headline and its second line hand the finding to a skimmer
2. Reading only the headers still tells the whole story
3. Every paragraph leads with or lands on a number
4. Changes appear as arrows
5. Dashes are gone, replaced by periods, arrows, or parentheses
6. Every [KUT] is written up or removed
7. The method box is present, with sources and scripts
8. A reader can finish in two minutes and recall three numbers
